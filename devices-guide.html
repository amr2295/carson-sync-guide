Devices Tab — Ops Reference Guide

Support Reference — Use this guide to understand the Devices tab, explain discrepancies to staff and buildings, and know when to escalate.

About the Devices Tab
Carson's Development team released a new "Devices" tab on the web platform that shows a property-level view of all smart locks at buildings using our Lyazon integration, as well as many (but not all) buildings using our Salto integration. When applicable, the tab shows all connected smart locks, their connectivity, and battery health.

This tab will ONLY display for buildings using: Lyazon • Salto (see section below for which Salto buildings)

This tab does NOT display statuses for: EagleEye Bridges/Cameras • Intercoms • Brivo Panels/Doors • DormaKaba Door Locks

Note: We do NOT (yet) send low-battery notifications to staff users.

What You're Looking At — Tab Overview
Lyazon Locks tab — admin.carson.live/devices/lyazon-locks
How Lyazon data is collected: Carson pings ALL Lyazon doorlocks every morning at 3:00 AM EST to retrieve and display the battery status. The Connected field updates in near real-time via keep-alives (up to ~15 min lag for disconnect detection).
Battery Color Coding
Battery health is displayed as a color-coded indicator in the Battery column. Here is what each color means for Lyazon:
Green — Battery > 25% (Good / Medium / Excellent)
Red — Battery 1%–25% (Low / Very Low / Critical)
Grey — No battery data available (null / unknown)
Lyazon threshold: 25% is the cutoff. Any lock at or below 25% battery shows Red. Anything above 25% shows Green. The summary counter "Low Battery" at the top of the page matches the number of Red locks.

Why do all my Salto doors show "Offline"?
This is a known characteristic of how Salto reports lock status to Carson. Salto does not push real-time connectivity data the same way Lyazon does. The "Offline" status does not mean the locks are broken or non-functional — it reflects a limitation in the integration's data reporting, not the physical lock state.
🔒 Data-on-card: Why Salto "Last Update" and battery may be stale
For Salto offline locks (common at most supported buildings), events and battery readings are not sent to the server in real time when a fob is used. Here's how the data gets updated:
  1. A resident or staff member uses a fob to tap the lock — the event and battery reading are stored on the fob
  2. That fob is later tapped on an online update reader — at that point, the data is uploaded to Salto Space and then reflected in Carson
What this means for Ops: If a building's fobs are rarely or never tapped on an update reader, the "Last Update" and battery state shown in Carson may be days or weeks old. BLE (Bluetooth key) access does provide more immediate updates. Do not interpret stale "Last Update" as proof that a lock has failed.

Discrepancy Talking Points — What to Say When Asked
This section gives Ops the language to use when a building contacts us with questions or concerns about what they see on the Devices tab.
What they say / askProviderTalking point
"A lock shows disconnected but it still has a battery percentage — how is that possible?" Lyazon The battery percentage shown is from the last successful ping (which happens every morning at 3am EST). When a lock goes offline after that, Carson retains the last known battery reading. The percentage is accurate as of the last time we could reach it — not necessarily right now.
"The 'Last Update' column shows a date from months ago — is that a problem?" Lyazon If a lock is not connected, the "Last Update" column reflects when it was last successfully reached — this is expected behavior. The "Last Online" column is more meaningful in that case. Please disregard "Last Update" for disconnected locks.
"A lock shows as online/connected but it says it's 'Offline' in the Offline Status filter — why?" Lyazon A lock is considered Offline if it hasn't checked in for more than 48 hours, even if "Connected" still shows true. The 48-hour rule is a derived state to catch stale connections. In this case, treat the lock as potentially unreachable and check "Last Online" for context.
"The battery shows Red at 19% — how urgent is that?" Lyazon Red means the battery is at or below 25% — it should be addressed soon. Advise building staff to schedule a battery replacement. We do not yet send automated low-battery alerts, so this tab is the best way to proactively catch these.
"What firmware should our locks be on?" Lyazon As of May 1, 2026, the current/most up-to-date firmware is 00.15.19. Newer firmware 00.18.07 is also being deployed. If your locks are on an older version, please note this and we can look into scheduling a firmware update.
"If a lock is on outdated firmware, will that degrade app performance?" Lyazon Outdated firmware can potentially affect reliability, but each case is different. The best step is to flag locks running significantly old firmware so we can assess whether an update is needed.
"The battery shows 'Normal' in green but I thought my locks had low battery?" Salto Salto battery states (Normal, Good, Medium, High, Low) reflect what was transmitted the last time the data synced — which for offline locks only happens when a fob is tapped on an online update reader. The reading may be stale. If you have a physical concern about a specific lock's battery, the most reliable check is a direct inspection or replacement.
"The 'Last Update' column looks different across all of my door locks — why?" Salto Salto updates happen at the individual door level, not all at once. Each lock's "Last Update" reflects when Carson last received an event from that specific lock. Differences between units are normal. Also note: depending on the building, the timestamp may be ahead or behind by a few minutes due to a known time-sync offset (see building details in Section 1).
"ALL of my doors show Offline — are they broken?" Salto No — the locks are not broken. The "Offline" status for Salto is a known data reporting limitation. Salto does not send real-time connectivity status to Carson the way Lyazon does. This does not mean the doors cannot open — it just means Carson doesn't have live connection visibility for Salto locks at this time.
"My building uses Salto but I don't see any of my locks on this tab" Salto Not all Salto buildings are currently supported. This tab works for Pearl House, The Frame, 1700 Jerome, and Flow Fort Lauderdale (limited doors). If your building is not one of these, the Devices tab will not show your locks. If you'd like to understand why or request integration, please email support@carson.live.
"What is the 'Lock Type' column? What does it mean?" Salto Lock Type refers to whether the Salto lock is an Online lock (directly connected to Salto's server and able to send near-real-time data) or an Offline lock (not directly connected — data only updates via fob sync through an update reader). Most supported buildings use Offline locks, which is why "Last Update" and battery may be stale. You can filter by Lock Type using the dropdown above the table.

Support Workflow — How to Use This Tab When Handling a Ticket
When someone contacts us about a door opening issue: Checking the Devices tab is a good early step in the process. It can confirm whether a lock is disconnected or has a low battery, which helps narrow down the cause.
Step-by-Step
  1. Navigate to Operations → Devices and select the appropriate tab (Lyazon Locks or Salto Locks)
  2. Use the Select Building filter to narrow to the building in question
  3. Find the specific unit/door and check the Connected and Battery State columns
  4. If the lock is disconnected: check "Last Online" to understand when it last had connectivity. If it's been a long time, escalate.
  5. If the battery is low (Red): advise building staff to replace the battery. Note: we do not (yet) send automated low-battery alerts.
  6. To export the current view for reporting or escalation: click the export/CSV button (top of table area). The export includes whatever filters and sorting you currently have applied.
Important caveats:
  • This tab works for Pearl House, The Frame, and ALL Lyazon buildings
  • At Lyazon buildings, this will only show Lyazon-managed doors. There may be other doors at the property controlled by Brivo, 2N, Akuvox, etc. that will NOT appear here
  • For Salto buildings, only the specific doors listed in Section 1 will appear
  • Salto battery and "Last Update" data for offline locks may be stale if building residents/staff have not recently synced fobs via an online update reader
For Staff Calling or Writing In About This Tab
  1. Confirm they are contacting us about an applicable building (Lyazon building, or Pearl House / The Frame / 1700 Jerome / Flow Fort Lauderdale for Salto)
  2. If inapplicable: let them know the Devices tab will not display their building's doorlock statuses. If they want to understand why or how to get their locks added to the Devices tab, direct them to email support@carson.live
  3. If applicable: use Sections 1 and 2 above to walk them through what they're seeing and address any discrepancies

FAQ — Frequently Asked Questions
Lyazon
What does it mean if a Lyazon door lock is "disconnected"?
A disconnected lock means that during the last daily 3am EST ping, Carson was unable to reach the lock via the Lyazon cloud. This could be due to a WiFi/network issue at the property, the lock hardware losing power, or a temporary Lyazon system issue. It does not automatically mean the lock is physically broken — but it does mean Carson cannot verify its current state. Check the "Last Online" column to see when it was last reachable.

Note: A lock can also be flagged as "Offline" (via the Offline Status filter) even if "Connected" shows true — this happens when the lock has not checked in for over 48 hours. Disconnect detection may also lag up to ~15 minutes due to keep-alive intervals.
How can a lock be disconnected and still display a battery level?
The battery percentage shown is from the last successful status ping. Carson stores that reading even after the lock goes offline. So if a lock was at 45% battery the last time Carson reached it, it will continue to show 45% until the lock reconnects and sends a new reading. Always treat the battery percentage of a disconnected lock as last known, not current.
What do the battery colors mean for Lyazon? What is the low-battery threshold?
Battery is displayed with a color indicator:
  • Green: Battery > 25% — healthy, no action needed
  • Red: Battery 1%–25% — low, schedule replacement soon
  • Grey: No battery data available (null or missing)
The "Low Battery" counter shown at the top of the Lyazon tab reflects the number of locks currently in Red state. The threshold is 25% and is set by Carson/Lyazon's integration mapping.
What firmware versions should my building's Lyazon doorlocks be on?
As of May 1, 2026, the current/most up-to-date firmware versions are 00.15.19 and 00.18.07 (00.18.07 is the newest and is actively being deployed). If your locks are running an older version, please note this and flag it — it can be addressed in a firmware update cycle.
If one of my locks is on outdated firmware, will that degrade app performance?
It can. Older firmware may have bugs or limitations that affect how reliably the lock communicates with the app. While it won't necessarily stop the lock from working, it's best practice to keep firmware up to date. If you're seeing issues with locks on old firmware, that's a signal worth escalating.
Can I sort, filter, or export the Lyazon table?
Yes. The Lyazon table supports:
  • Sorting: Click any column header to sort ASC → DESC → off. You can stack sorts across multiple columns.
  • Filtering: Use the dropdowns above the table (Select Building, Connection Status, Battery State, Offline Status) to narrow results.
  • Column configuration: Click the "Columns" button (top right of the table) to show/hide columns or drag to reorder them.
  • Export/CSV: Use the export button to download the current view as a CSV file. The export reflects whatever filters and sorting you have applied.
Salto
What do the battery colors and states mean for Salto?
Salto battery is shown as a state label (no percentage) with a color indicator:
  • Green: Good / Normal / Medium / High — battery is 33%–100%
  • Red: Low / Run out — battery is 0%–33%
  • Grey: Null / Unknown — no telemetry data available
Note: Salto percentages are approximate ranges, not exact readings. Salto does not send percentage values to Carson — only the state label.
Why does the "Last Update" column look different across all of my door locks?
Each door lock sends data to Carson independently when it registers an event (like a door opening or a status update). There's no synchronized batch update like with Lyazon. So each lock will show a different "Last Update" based on its own activity. Additionally, note the known time offsets: Pearl House shows timestamps ~1hr 2min ahead of Salto's own logs, and The Frame shows timestamps ~1–3 minutes behind. This is a known quirk of the integration, not a data error.
Why is Salto "Last Update" sometimes weeks or months old?
For offline Salto locks (most common at supported buildings), data updates happen through a data-on-card process:
  1. A resident or staff member taps an offline lock with a fob — the event and battery reading are stored on the fob
  2. That fob must then be tapped on an online update reader for the data to be sent to Salto Space and then Carson
If fobs in the building are rarely or never synced via an update reader, the "Last Update" and battery state in Carson can become significantly stale. This is expected behavior for offline Salto locks, not a bug. BLE (Bluetooth key) usage provides more timely updates.
I click into my Devices tab and ALL of my doors show "Offline" — why? What can we do?
The "Offline" status for Salto locks is a known data reporting limitation of the current Salto ↔ Carson integration. Salto does not push real-time connectivity status to Carson the way Lyazon does. This does not mean your locks are non-functional — your residents and staff can still open doors normally. If you are experiencing actual door-opening failures, please contact support. If you'd like real-time status visibility for your Salto locks to be improved, please email support@carson.live so we can log your request with the product team.
What is "Lock Type" and why does it matter?
Lock Type classifies whether a Salto lock is:
  • Online: The lock is directly connected to Salto's server and can send near-real-time data (battery, events)
  • Offline: The lock is not directly connected — data is only transmitted when a fob is synced via an online update reader (data-on-card)
Most buildings supported in Carson currently use offline locks. This is why "Last Update" and battery may be stale, and why all doors show "Offline" connectivity status. You can filter by Lock Type using the dropdown above the table.
Can I sort, filter, or export the Salto table?
Yes. The Salto table supports:
  • Sorting: Click any column header to sort ASC → DESC → off.
  • Filtering: Use the dropdowns above the table (Select Building, Battery State, Lock Type) to narrow results.
  • Column configuration: Click the "Columns" button (top right of the table) to show/hide columns or drag to reorder them.
  • Export/CSV: Use the export button to download the current view as a CSV file. The export reflects whatever filters and sorting you have applied.
