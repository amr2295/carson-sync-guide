Devices Tab — Ops Reference Guide

Support Reference — Use this guide to understand the Devices tab, explain discrepancies to staff and buildings, and know when to escalate.

About the Devices Tab
Carson’s Development team released a new “Devices” tab on the web platform that shows a property-level view of all smart locks at buildings using our Lyazon integration, as well as many (but not all) buildings using our Salto integration. When applicable, the tab shows all connected smart locks, their connectivity, and battery health.

This tab will ONLY display for buildings using: Lyazon • Salto (see section below for which Salto buildings)

This tab does NOT display statuses for: EagleEye Bridges/Cameras • Intercoms • Brivo Panels/Doors • DormaKaba Door Locks

Note: We do NOT (yet) send low-battery notifications to staff users.

Why do all my Salto doors show “Offline”?
This is a known characteristic of how Salto reports lock status to Carson. Salto does not push real-time connectivity data the same way Lyazon does. The “Offline” status does not mean the locks are broken — it reflects a limitation in the integration’s data reporting, not the physical lock state.

Discrepancy Talking Points — What to Say When Asked
This section gives Ops the language to use when a building contacts us with questions or concerns about what they see on the Devices tab.
What they say / askProviderTalking point
“A lock shows disconnected but it still has a battery % — how?” Lyazon The battery % shown is from the last successful 3am ping. When a lock goes offline after that, Carson retains the last known reading. The % is accurate as of the last time we could reach it — not necessarily right now.
“The ‘Last Update’ shows a date from months ago — is that a problem?” Lyazon If a lock is not connected, “Last Update” reflects when it was last successfully reached. Use the “Last Online” column instead. Disregard “Last Updated” for disconnected locks.
“What firmware should our locks be on?” Lyazon As of May 1, 2026, current firmware is 00.15.19. Newest: 00.18.07 (actively deploying). Older versions can be flagged for a firmware update cycle.
“If a lock is on outdated firmware, will that degrade app performance?” Lyazon Potentially. Older firmware may have reliability issues. Best practice is to keep firmware up to date. Flag locks on significantly older firmware for an update assessment.
“The ‘Last Update’ column looks different across all of my door locks — why?” Salto Each lock sends data independently when it registers an event. Differences between units are normal. Note: Pearl House shows timestamps ~1hr 2min ahead; The Frame ~1–3 min behind. Known quirk of the integration.
“ALL of my doors show Offline — are they broken?” Salto No — the locks are not broken. The “Offline” status is a known data reporting limitation. Salto does not send real-time connectivity status to Carson. Doors can still open normally. For actual door-opening failures, escalate via support.
“My building uses Salto but I don’t see any of my locks” Salto Not all Salto buildings are currently supported. Support Workflow — How to Use This Tab When Handling a Ticket
When someone contacts us about a door opening issue: Checking the Devices tab is a good early step. It can confirm whether a lock is disconnected or has a low battery, which helps narrow down the cause.
Step-by-Step
  1. Navigate to Operations → Devices and select the appropriate tab (Lyazon Locks or Salto Locks)
  2. Use the Select Building filter to narrow to the building in question
  3. Find the specific unit/door and check the Connected and Battery State columns
  4. If the lock is disconnected: check “Last Online” to understand when it last had connectivity. If it’s been a long time, escalate.
  5. If the battery is low: advise building staff to replace the battery. Note: we do not (yet) send automated low-battery alerts.
Important caveats:
  • This tab works for Pearl House, The Frame, and ALL Lyazon buildings
  • At Lyazon buildings, this will only show Lyazon-managed doors. Other doors at the property (Brivo, 2N, Akuvox, etc.) will NOT appear here
  • For Salto buildings, only the specific doors listed in Section 1 will appear
For Staff Calling or Writing In About This Tab
  1. Confirm they are contacting us about an applicable building (Lyazon building, or Pearl House / The Frame / 1700 Jerome / Flow Fort Lauderdale for Salto)
  2. If inapplicable: let them know the Devices tab won’t show their doorlock statuses. If they want to understand why or get their locks added, direct to support@carson.live
  3. If applicable: use Sections 1 and 2 above to walk them through what they’re seeing and address discrepancies

FAQ — Frequently Asked Questions
Lyazon
What does it mean if a Lyazon door lock is “disconnected”?
A disconnected lock means that during the last daily 3am EST ping, Carson was unable to reach the lock via the Lyazon cloud. This could be due to a WiFi/network issue at the property, the lock hardware losing power, or a temporary Lyazon system issue. It does not automatically mean the lock is physically broken. Check the “Last Online” column to see when it was last reachable.
How can a lock be disconnected and still display a battery level?
The battery percentage shown is from the last successful status ping. Carson stores that reading even after the lock goes offline. If a lock was at 45% the last time Carson reached it, it will continue to show 45% until it reconnects and sends a new reading. Always treat the battery % of a disconnected lock as last known, not current.
What firmware versions should my building’s Lyazon doorlocks be on?
As of May 1, 2026, current firmware is 00.15.19 and newest is 00.18.07 (actively being deployed). If locks are running an older version, please flag it — it can be addressed in a firmware update cycle.
If one of my locks is on outdated firmware, will that degrade app performance?
It can. Older firmware may have bugs or limitations that affect how reliably the lock communicates with the app. While it won’t necessarily stop the lock from working, it’s best practice to keep firmware up to date. If you’re seeing issues with locks on old firmware, escalate for an assessment.
Salto
Why does the “Last Update” column look different across all of my door locks?
Each door lock sends data to Carson independently when it registers an event. There’s no synchronized batch update like Lyazon. So each lock will show a different “Last Update” based on its own activity. Note the known offsets: Pearl House shows timestamps ~1hr 2min ahead of Salto’s own logs; The Frame shows ~1–3 minutes behind. This is a known quirk, not a data error.
I click into my Devices tab and ALL of my doors show “Offline” — why? What can we do?
The “Offline” status for Salto locks is a known data reporting limitation of the current Salto ↔ Carson integration. Salto does not push real-time connectivity status to Carson the way Lyazon does. This does not mean your locks are non-functional — residents and staff can still open doors normally. For actual door-opening failures, contact support. To request improved real-time visibility, email support@carson.live.
